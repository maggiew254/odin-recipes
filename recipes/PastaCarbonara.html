Pasta Carbonara Recipe

Description

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons salt
  • 4 oz. bacon or pancetta
  • 2 oz. Parmesan cheese
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 lb. spaghetti
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Steps

Makes 4 servings

  1. Heat 6 qt. water in a large pot over high. When water starts to steam, add 3 Tbsp. salt and cover pot with a lid.
  2. Remove 4 oz. bacon from packaging and cut into about 1x¼" strips.
  3. Finely grate 2 oz. cheese and set aside one-quarter of cheese for later.
  4. Whisk 4 egg yolks and 2 whole eggs in a medium bowl until no streaks remain, then stir in remaining grated cheese.
  5. Add several cranks of pepper and set aside.
  6. heat 2 Tbsp. oil in a large heavy pot over medium.
  7. Add bacon and cook, stirring occasionally, until crisp around the edges, 7–10 minutes.
  8. Remove pot from heat. Using a wooden spoon, fish out bacon and transfer to a small bowl.
  9. Pour fat into a heatproof measuring cup, then add back about 3 Tbsp. to pot.
  10. Cook 1 lb. pasta in boiling water, stirring occasionally. Just before pasta is done, scoop out 1¾ cups of pasta cooking liquid.
  11. Add 1 cup reserved pasta cooking liquid to pot and bring to a boil over medium-high. Drain pasta in a colander, then transfer to pot.
  12. Cook pasta, stirring constantly, until al dente and water is reduced by about half, about 2 minutes. Remove pot from heat.
  13. Whisk ¼ cup pasta cooking liquid into reserved egg mixture, then very slowly stream into pot, stirring constantly, until cheese is melted and egg is thickened to form a glossy sauce.
  14. Mix in bacon and top with pepper and reserved cheese.
